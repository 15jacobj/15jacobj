About me

Computing has been a lifelong hobby which I have always enjoyed greatly. Unfortunately, my experience with computing in education has been largely underwhelming and led me to drop the subject. At the moment I am working towards my front-end development certification on freeCodeCamp and to that end I have created this website, my first ever portfolio. My first experience with computing was learning basic html several years ago. Since then I have dabbled in various fields including embedded technologies (spark core) and Arduino-based projects programmed largely using the wiring framework for C++. I have also used the raspberry pi with limited success. At this point I was barely scratching the surface, and soon, I realised that it was better to learn something more simple in depth than adopt a "copy-paste", results based attitude. from then on I concentrated more on starting from the beginning,and went back to my HTML roots, picking up a little CSS using the Bootstrap framework along the way.
As I am as-yet unqualified, professional work has been practically non-existent. My first (and only) major website was creating a portfolio site for London-based film and television writer/director Jonathan Jones, which I provided gratis. I am still the admin for it to this day, and while it does undeniably have the look and feel of a first website, I am still very proud of the immense work I put in.
My ambitions for the future are to become a fully qualified (front and back-end) developer before I reach university and, by then to be proficient in several major languages to give me the grounding for a career in the field, should I choose it. Stay posted as I am sure I will have plenty of interesting projects coming soon!
